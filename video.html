Kazakhstan is the largest country in Central Asia, ranking 9th in the world by area. It has a variety of natural landscapes, including steppes, mountains and deserts. The capital is Nursultan (formerly Astana). Kazakhstan has a rich cultural heritage, including many ethnic groups and traditions. The country's economy is based on natural resources such as oil and gas.

Almaty is the largest city in Kazakhstan, located in the southeastern part of the country at the foot of the Tien Shan Mountains. It is a cultural and economic center known for its parks, architecture and developed infrastructure. The city combines modern and historical elements, and is also famous for its picturesque natural landscapes. Almaty is an important transport hub and a popular tourist destination.

Astana (Nur Sultan) is the capital of Kazakhstan, located in the central part of the country. The city was renamed Nursultan in 2019 in honor of the first president Nazarbayev. Astana is known for its modern architecture, including famous buildings such as Baiterek and Khan Shatyr. The city is actively developing and becoming an important political, cultural and economic center of Kazakhstan.
